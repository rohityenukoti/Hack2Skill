**Overview**  
Build with AI: Code for Communities is a national hackathon by Google Cloud that connects developers, designers, and AI builders directly with sitting **Members of Parliament** to solve real, on-the-ground governance problems in constituency development planning, environment, healthcare, and agriculture.  
Every problem statement here was submitted by an MP's office, describing a gap they are actively trying to close for their constituents right now. The best solutions won't just win a prize pool — they'll be piloted in a PHC, a constituency, or a district, with a possibility to scale if the pilot works.  
The top teams will travel to an **in-person demo day in New Delhi**, where you'll present in front of the MPs and other industry experts. If you've ever wanted your code to do more than get a good demo reaction — this is that chance.

## **What this hackathon is about**

We believe AI has the potential to streamline frontline systems across healthcare, farm advisories, pollution monitoring and more, many of which still run largely on paper registers and phone calls.

**Build with AI: Code for Communities** hackathon asks builders to close that gap using AI as the actual engine that turns scattered, unstructured citizen input and fragmented public data into something an MP, a district health officer, or a farmer can act on today.

## **Smart Health**

AI-Driven Health Center & Supply Chain Management

**The Problem**

PHCs and CHCs face recurring operational gaps — medicine stock-outs, unmanaged patient footfall, bed unavailability, unpredictable doctor attendance — tracked manually with no real-time visibility, leading to shortages and under-resourced facilities.

**The Challenge**  
Build a multilingual AI platform for real-time health centre management covering stock monitoring, patient footfall, bed availability, doctor attendance, and test availability audits. It should generate early stock-out warnings, AI-driven demand forecasts, and smart resource redistribution recommendations across a district's PHCs/CHCs — and automatically flag underperforming or under-resourced centres to district administrators for intervention.

## **Tools & Tech You Can Use**

You're free to build with any stack, but the following are recommended and supported with documentation/mentor familiarity (and credits, where available):

#### **AI/ML & generative AI**

Gemini API, Vertex AI (model building, fine-tuning, agents), Google AI Studio

#### **Language & voice**

Cloud Speech-to-Text and Text-to-Speech (for multilingual/voice intake), Dialogflow (for voice/SMS conversational flows), Translation API

#### **Vision**

Vertex AI Vision / Gemini multimodal (for analyzing citizen-uploaded photos — smoke, crop disease, infrastructure issues), MediaPipe

#### **Geospatial & mapping**

Google Maps Platform (for hotspot mapping in Tracks 1 and 2), Earth Engine (for satellite imagery in Tracks 2 and 4\)

#### **Data & backend**

BigQuery (for combining demographic/public datasets), Firebase (auth, real-time DB, hosting for fast prototyping), Cloud Run / Cloud Functions

#### **Apps**

Flutter or Android for citizen-facing mobile apps; WhatsApp Business API or basic SMS gateways for low-connectivity access

#### **Public data**

data.gov.in, Census/NFHS datasets, CPCB air quality data, IMD weather data, state agriculture department datasets — explore what's relevant to your track

## **Evaluation Parameters**

Submissions will be judged across:

#### **Problem-Solution Fit**

**20%**

Does it directly address the stated problem, with the right scope?

#### **AI/Technical Execution**

**25%**

Is the AI doing real work (not decorative)? Does the prototype function end-to-end?

#### **Deployability & Scalability**

**25%**

Could this realistically run in a real PHC/constituency/district in weeks, and scale beyond one pilot site?

#### **Inclusivity & Accessibility**

**15%**

Multilingual support, voice/low-literacy access, low-connectivity design where relevant

#### **Impact Potential**

**10%**

Scale of benefit — how many citizens, how meaningfully?

#### **Presentation & Clarity**

**5%**

Can a non-technical MP's office understand the value in 5 minutes?
